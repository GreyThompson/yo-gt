Dominican Republic Project

To launch spinal care treatmens in the town of Moca in the Dominican Republic.

World Spine Care is currently working on the development of a project in the town of Moca in the Dominican Republic.

The Project aims to:

  • Perform an epidemiological study to determine the impact of Spinal Diseases;
  • Disorders in designated rural or underserved communities;
  • Identify health care resources in rural and underserved communities, which could be considered in the establishment of an evidence-based Spine care program;
  • Establish Spine Care Centers in rural or underserved communities in Dominican Republic;
  • Recruit volunteer health care professionals to provide primary Spine care; and
  • Ensure interaction of health professionals with local care providers and traditional healers to share knowledge on the assessment and management of spinal problems and harmonize treatment efforts.

The expected outcomes of the Project are:

  • Improved health and healthcare of people with Spinal Disorders and Injuries in rural and underserved communities;
  • Trained and skilled individuals on Spine care from the targeted rural and underserved communities; and
  • Ongoing and sustainable Spine care in the targeted rural and underserved communities and the establishment of similar programs in other communities.

The initial Project target area are:

  • Moca; and
  • Cayetano Germosen.
